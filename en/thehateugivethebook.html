  • Angie Thomas has succeeded in creating a story that has left an indelible mark on contemporary literature and will continue to inspire readers to think and act.

  • Angie Thomas

    The Hate U Give, novel written by Angie THOMAS, born September 20, 1988 in Jackson, Mississippi, is an American novelist. The novel was published in 2017, then adapted for film under the title The Hate U Give: La Haine qu'on donne in 2018.

  • Book's extract

    ”However, I believe that one day that will change. How ? I don't know. When ? I know even less. So why ? Because there will always be someone to fight for. And maybe now it's my turn. Others are fighting too, even in Garden Heights where we sometimes have the impression that there is not much to defend. People realize, shout, demonstrate, demand. They don't forget. I think that's the most important thing. Khalil, I will never forget him. I'll never give up. I will never shut up. I promise."

  • Book inspiration

    This is not just the story of a young girl who sees her childhood friend shot dead by the police, it is also the story of a young girl who finds her voice and the cause she will defend.

    Angie THOMAS found the inspiration for this novel in 2010, just after the case of Oscar Grant, a young man shot dead by the police.

    Tupac is an important figure in the book, the title “the hate u give” comes from the famous tattoo he had on his stomach “THUGLIFE” which means “The hate you to the little infants fucks everybody”, “the hatred that we give the children fuck the world up”

  • Tupac Shakur

    Identifying Starr and Seven's characters as roses that grew in harsh environments is a powerful metaphor that reflects their resilience and ability to thrive despite the adverse conditions around them. “Roses can grow in harsh environments and still bloom,” this metaphor was inspired by Tupac Shakur’s poem “The Rose That Grew From Concrete”
