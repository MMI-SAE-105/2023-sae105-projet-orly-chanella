THe Hate U Give

Immerse yourself in the powerful and emotional world of “The Hate U Give”, discover how the captivating story highlights important issues such as social injustice, diversity, the power of youth and the fight for equality.

    Main character Starr Carter, a teenager living between a neighborhood that is often described as a ghetto and an upscale high school, witnesses the murder of her friend Khalil by a police officer. The novel follows his quest for justice and the evolution of his voice.

    Starr managed to go out into her comfort zone and spoke in front of her comrades, she managed to convey her anger, her frustration, her determination and decided to speak out against injustice, she was a figure of inspiration for those who knew how to listen to it.

    The title of Angie Thomas' film and novel refers to the concept THUG LIFE - the acronym for The Hate U Give Little Infants Fucks Everybody - developed by the rapper Tupac Shakur.

    “If no one makes the decision to put an end to this negative spiral, nothing will ever change”, this film is a metaphor for this concept.

Bringing a Powerful Story to Life

The film adaptation of “The Hate U Give” by Angie Thomas was directed by George Tillman Jr.. The story follows Starr Carter, a teenager torn between two worlds. When she witnesses the murder of her friend by a police officer, her life is turned upside down, forcing her to choose between remaining silent and fighting for justice. The film stays true to the themes of the book, exploring police brutality, racial injustice and identity. It stars Amandla Stenberg, who plays the character of Starr brilliantly. The adaptation broadens the impact of the book by reaching a wider audience through emotionally powerful scenes and the director's skillful use of music.
